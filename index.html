CoViD19 logistic function fitting

The figures below with the sigmoid curves show the cumulated confirmed COVID19 cases in various geographic segments fitted to a logistic function in order to determine 1) midpoint (day with peak number of new cases), 2) future maximum number of cumulated cases and 3) steepness of the curve. When a larger percentage of the population is tested, then the data points and hence the data fitting becomes more reliable. The quality of the fitted models is highly dependent on the quality of the input data. If the data cannot be fitted, then it could among other things be a sign of 1) incorrect data due to insufficient testing, 2) too few cases, 3) government intervention/quarantines preventing the natural propagation of the virus, 1) deliberately falsified data. The code to generate the colorblind optimized plots below is available on GitHub. The data was downloaded from the European Center for Disease Prevention and Control (ECDC) here. Figures for China are not included, as the validity of the data has been questioned.
